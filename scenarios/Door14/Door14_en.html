Opening door 14 is easy, but dealing with the heroism of these enemies...

My lord, you want to deal with the Caliph yourself? Please take fireproof clothing. This guy is, no joke, a first-class pyromancer.

This map is split by a mountain range from the left middle part right to your doorstep. On that mountain, next to you, sits Caliph. He is a nuisance that has to be dealt with right away. He is supported by his little brother, Sultan Abdul, whose weird tomato-shaped castle spits out Arabian swordsmen as if there were no tomorrow.

To top it all off, Phillip shoots at you with mangonels and Emir in the top left is starting to build a large castle.

Hint 1 Your king is almost fireproof compared to your other units. He can trigger most of the Caliph’s fire traps with little harm.
Hint 2 Destroying the Caliph’s mercenary post will stop him from recruiting more units in the near future.
Hint 3 After killing the Caliph you gain access to iron and farmland. Only take what you can protect. Sometimes market self-sufficiency is cheaper than maintaining outside income.
Hint 4 The best way to deal with mangonels is not to deal with them at all: stay out of their range.
Hint 5 If you cannot gun down all those knights and swordsmen, maintain a melee ground army that can stack up and withstand their attacks.
Hint 6 Dealing with the Sultan first gives you a nice vantage point over Emir and unlocks many more resources.
Hint 7 A fire department against fire ballistae and slaves setting your buildings on fire is a good idea.
