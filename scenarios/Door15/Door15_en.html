You have to pry open door 15. It might have been stuck in the frame. I hope you spend a lot of time on this glorious multilayered map. Such a bastard to create.

You continue the story from Door8 and must enter the Land of Production by passing the great wall. The gate is protected by Frederick beside two corporals of the Wolf. They plot against you from their headquarters, represented by the light blue pieces on a small mountain behind the wall.

The wall is large and well manned. Not right now, because you just arrived, but troops are flooding it as we speak. They have also signed a contract to outpay you for any shooting unit you might want, and they will spend anything on defenses to keep you out.

Your army is split in half. You brought a large force from your last adventure, while the abandoned keep you may want to claim is occupied by bandits. There is a tower to refurbish and an engineer’s guild still intact, though it is currently burning.

Aside from that, the Vulture in the bottom left focuses on ranged units from his mountain, while Richard ‘The Desperate’ in the bottom right is having a hard time fighting everyone else. Can you master the art of war? Have fun and good luck!

Hint 1 Control groups let you stack your ground army. Send each group to the same spot so shields cover more troops at once.
Hint 2 You fit 3 apple orchards on the farm spot behind your keep. Alternate them in seasons for maximum food, but remember you may need bread for a bigger population.
Hint 3 You can use workers besides woodcutters before clearing the keep entrance. That lets you put out the engineer guild fire.
Hint 4 Do not lose too many ranged units at the start, or defending your economy until Richard and the Vulture die becomes hard.
Hint 5 Salvaging a tower always returns half its stone. Sometimes salvaging and rebuilding is cheaper than repairing.
Hint 6 With 8 shields on a big square tower, placed two per side near the edges, you can protect troops from all directions.
Hint 7 More towers give you room to reposition shields and troops.
Hint 8 If you wait too long to kill the Vulture or Richard, the unit limit will constrain recruitment. Move fast.
Hint 9 A trebuchet and catapult line shoot from Richard’s side. Build a new tower if your existing one cannot reach them.
Hint 10 Taking out the Vulture after setting up camp gives you easily defendable resources.
Hint 11 Keeping some starting fire throwers alive deals with most ground forces easily.
Hint 12 Bringing down the wall is the main challenge. Siege towers on one side, shields and ground troops over the wall are recommended.
Hint 13 Use your 4 fire ballistae and horse archers with many shields to shoot down fire throwers before marching in.
Hint 14 Use shields for every force. Multiple shield control groups help keep them with your units.
Hint 15 Set their castle on fire to stop reinforcements.
Hint 16 Take out only as many troops as needed before heading to the headquarters. You can also break the wall with troops to let ranged units through.
Hint 17 Apothecaries can heal siege equipment. Odd, but it works.
