The first thing you notice when looking at today’s map is the similarity to door 7. Two parties are fighting each other, catching you in their crossfire.

Next to you are two Rats who definitely think you are an easy victim. Further away in the bottom right is a Snake. The Sheriff and the purple Pig form a party with the Snake and the orange Rat. Besides that orange Rat, all other lords on your side of the river form the opposing party. Phillip in the top left claims the whole corner as his own.

Most farmland is in the middle of the map, giving you nearly no natural access to resources. You do have a market though, and heck yeah, you will use that to your advantage. You start while defending attacks from both Rats, so take a good look at the preview and the buildings already set up for you.

Hint 1 Do not mess with the Rats’ arrow slits. They definitely cheat. The best way to handle their cheeky tactics is to avoid them.
Hint 2 Who is the biggest lord? If you surpass the Rats on that list, Snake and Phillip will pressure you a lot less.
Hint 3 Your starting stone is best invested into closing your castle and building a big tower with a tower ballista against siege engines.
Hint 4 Buying wood, iron, flour and beer constantly will keep your economy flowing. If you need more economy, set up more production buildings.
Hint 5 -5 fear factor will guarantee profit from processing flour and iron.
Hint 6 Because your population is contained inside your small castle, one more chapel already grants +2 religion. Maybe the tortured priests simply work faster.
Hint 7 You can save stone by only fixing the wall that is already completely broken down, and by using low wall for it.
Hint 8 You can recycle the gatehouse and towers for more stone. Some other buildings may also be more useful recycled than running.
Hint 9 If you can afford a cathedral, monks can fend off incoming ground troops while you sell swords and armor for profit.
