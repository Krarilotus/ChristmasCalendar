You came prepared. Knowing the difficulty by now, you opened door 20 with caution, saw a vantage point and set up a base before the enemies arrived. Now they have to run against your cliffs and walls.

Bottom left, at your hill’s biggest opening, Richard arrives with green pieces. Top left, the black Caliph will make life difficult. Near him is the Rat, and top right Frederick, who starts slower than the Rat but will strike hard eventually. Bottom right, Emir and Sultan share a corner breeding Arabian swordsmen.

Resources are abundant: farmland, stone and iron are scattered around the hill. Economically you should be fine. But can you keep up with six enemies?

Hint 1 Consider rushing Richard again. It gives freedom in one direction.
Hint 2 Do not forget your early resource setup, even while rushing one or two enemies.
Hint 3 Your knights can slow early enemy economies.
Hint 4 Wall off your entrances to prevent retaliation against your economy.
Hint 5 Do not overspend defending every tower from mangonels. Defend what you can and focus the entrance most AIs use to reach your keep.
Hint 6 Richard has a huge bounty. Remember how to lure out his lord?
Hint 7 Fire ballistae are excellent against single heavy units like knights.
Hint 8 Fire in general is the strongest defense mechanic.
Hint 9 After Richard falls, expand downward and build towers farther from your castle.
Hint 10 Caliph fire ballistae hurt tower units, but he rebuilds slowly if his gold stays low.
Hint 11 Many mangonels focused on one AI can keep its economy low.
Hint 12 Your initial 3 shields can secure the side of your choice against ranged attacks.
Hint 13 Juggle shields so one heals at apothecaries while others work.
Hint 14 At army limit you can still replace cheap units with better ones.
Hint 15 Under heavy catapult fire, any AI will fall.
Hint 16 Improve your strategy every try. Do not stop at what works; make it work better.
