Suddenly, light shines through the sixteenth door. You do not fall to your knees to worship it; you walk through and find yourself on a map with three others who could not withstand the light.

Now they want to force you to join their religion. Not peacefully, but with catapults, monks, and some painful swordsmen. You only have a few fletchers and need a plan to counter them.

The mountains split the land with two broad passages. Your side has some farm spots and resources, while the three Abbots share a huge oasis around a pond. They have not set up their huge fortresses yet, so you have time to prepare. When they strike, they hit hard.

Hint 1 Your main economy will once again get its bread and butter... wait yes, its bread. Build a bread economy.
Hint 2 Do not get your cathedral too late. Matching their melee troops with monks is crucial.
Hint 3 Buying wood early is great. You can also cut one row outside your moat before it finishes, while your own trees keep growing.
Hint 4 Ale and inns are cheaper popularity than food. With a cathedral, half rations can even be worth it.
Hint 5 You can take 3 relatively safe quarries, 2 iron mines and 3 farms. Take them early and protect them as long as possible.
Hint 6 Negative fear factor can increase your limited bow output, but it weakens archer damage.
Hint 7 Need more ranged units? Use more tower siege engines.
Hint 8 Many AIs struggle with moat, but they will dig through if your wall is complete.
Hint 9 Spearmen can kill early archers from the top pass, letting you group archers at the bottom.
Hint 10 Spearmen and monks run and stack faster in aggressive stance.
