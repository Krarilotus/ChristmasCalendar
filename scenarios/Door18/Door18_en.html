The last week has started as you open door 18. Impressive how you completed each map in one day, honest and fair, leading you here.

This was the third map designed for the calendar. It was once meant for door three, but testers called it difficult. So it was enhanced into a real challenge. What did you expect? The difficulty rises each day.

You are surrounded and in a bad spot. At least you have a 1.25 tax income multiplier. The only way out is through Frederick. You have a decent, not optimal castle, a big army and lots of gold. Sounds easy? Prove it.

Do not skip the preview image. Figure out where everything is before charging into battle.

Hint 1 Do not exploit overcrowding watchtowers by sending units onto nearby walls. If you overcrowd, at least send them while unmanned.
Hint 2 Invest into 1-2 large towers to shoot at the Caliphs, or your watchtower troops will not last.
Hint 3 You have exactly 4 shields. Position them wisely.
Hint 4 Your battering ram destroyed Frederick’s front gate. It can destroy more if protected.
Hint 5 Use knights to deal with roaming archers and crossbowmen.
Hint 6 Knights can defuse most pitch engineers and traps.
Hint 7 Pikemen and macemen are good tower clearers. Get them onto Frederick’s walls.
Hint 8 Use limited engineers for fire ballistae or a siege tower, and maybe defensive engines against Caliph fire ballistae.
Hint 9 Your three trebuchets can devastate enemy economies.
Hint 10 Destroy Frederick’s barracks to stop him spending gold on troops for the walls.
Hint 11 Delete unused buildings, set up fire defense and build outside walls against fire.
Hint 12 Caliphs only build fire ballistae above a gold threshold, around 350. Keep their gold low.
Hint 13 Remove walls or add stairs in front of your keep to improve troop pathing.
Hint 14 After Frederick dies, harass Caliph economy with knights, assassins, slaves and horse archers.
Hint 15 Swordsmen and assassins are best for killing lords. Reserve them for final blows.
