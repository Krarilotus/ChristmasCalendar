Did you peek early? Or is it time to open door 4 already?

In this map you are facing a concurrent thread of assassins looming around your territory and climbing your castle walls, while you yourself have to rely on the mercenary troops to defend yourself. It seems pretty straight forward, but how can you deal with those invisible slayers, punching you where you expect them the least? It is still a challenge of strategic thinking after all. What is important? Where to take an advantage and which positions to avoid?

That is exactly, why you should take a look at the preview image above before heading into the chaos. The top left and the bottom right are occupied by silently sneaking Nizars in camouflaging green and black. Emir, the other thread, is coming at you with the blue pieces from the top right. The swamp around you will slow down units, which will make your archers miss occasionally.

As this might be your first map to attempt at least twice. I can shorten your time spent, if you are out of ideas with these useful insights:

Hint 1 You will have to decide if rapidly replacing raided outside economy buildings or defending those with costly units is more efficient.
Hint 2 A partly defended position is just a waste of troops.
Hint 3 A flourishing and healthy economy evolves around getting your wood production up first, then investing into other production chains.
Hint 4 Fire ballistae can be dealt with more easily by manually targeting them with your tower ballistae.
Hint 5 As your enemies have 3 corners of the map, expanding your position towards the 4th corner will yield easier economical advancements.
Hint 6 Enclosing your castle will only work out for you, if you can utilize your positional advantage through fire throwers, oil engineers and other traps. Hence, invest your gold and resources first into troops then into your castle.
Hint 7 Buying wheat while selling bread will result in profit.

Enjoy the game. Or as some might say: happy sneaking!
Good luck and have fun.
