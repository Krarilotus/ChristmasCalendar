You made it: door 21. This is the last map of the calendar published in 2019. Any map after this would be made in 2020, because quality should not drop at this difficulty.

This is the first map that introduces certain death. A clock is ticking. Richard has captured your lord and Sultan’s lord. He and Frederick are torturing you to death in an assassin-proof cage in the top right. Can you kill Richard and free both lords in time?

Recruitment and economy are very limited. Your population is capped by existing hovels, so losing hovels means losing workers and recruits. You and Sultan share a city bottom left. Richard shields the prison with his castle, Frederick sends patrols from the top left, the Abbot blocks the front of your city, and the Rat controls the right side with archers.

The AIs will still attack your keep, but may wait confused because your lord is missing. Normally walling in your lord is forbidden as bug abuse; here it is an exception until you free him. You have one single fletcher. Can you still win?

Hint 1 Rushing the Abbot is inevitable. The question is only how much it costs.
Hint 2 Assassins move faster to a rally point than when controlled directly.
Hint 3 After setting an assassin rally point, select them and use stop to instantly redirect them to the new rally point.
Hint 4 To set a rally point, select the recruitment building, press the unit number, then click the map.
Hint 5 Use hotkeys for faster building access. The manual or Steam hotkey guide can help.
Hint 6 When rushing the Abbot, climb the gate with a few assassins, then rally the rest to his keep.
Hint 7 Taking out the Rat with minimum effort rewards you with easier farmland.
Hint 8 Position your lord behind or on Sultan’s lord to change torturer focus.
Hint 9 Both lords die by July 1190 at the latest. Kill Richard and secure the area before then.
Hint 10 Ask Sultan for goods as often as possible, especially stone to sell.
Hint 11 Tax aggressively but do not leave popularity too low.
Hint 12 Relocate stockpile, armoury and granary at the start for efficient production chains.
Hint 13 Never let wheat run dry. Expand bread economy as much as possible.
Hint 14 Use every idle worker to get the economy curve right.
Hint 15 Frederick attacks the target with most gold. Spend gold so Sultan takes the beating.
Hint 16 You need about 300 assassins for the final assault against Richard. Start recruiting early.
Hint 17 Disable buildings to free recruits for the army when the time comes.
Hint 18 Use positive fear factor only for crucial fights; delete and rebuild fear buildings around timing windows.
Hint 19 For the final assault, remove tower archers somehow.
Hint 20 Use attack-here with assassins on buildings if they would otherwise climb walls.
Hint 21 Attack from all 4 sides to kill towers at the same time.
Hint 22 Prepare a few assassins for each pitch engineer to defuse pitch before the main force moves in.
Hint 23 If timings align, Richard leaves with his second attack right when you should strike. Move in after he leaves.
Hint 24 Slow game speed to 20 or lower if army micro is too hard.
Hint 25 Keep Sultan’s lord alive to handle Frederick after Richard dies.
Hint 26 Sultan’s lord will not return to his keep. Guard him and take the gold lead so Frederick targets you.
Hint 27 Send Sultan gold to produce troops, and ask for assistance before he attacks so he stacks forces at your keep.
